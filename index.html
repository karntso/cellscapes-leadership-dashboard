Brand FAQs

How should we be talking about ourselves moving forward?
No more "Allen Institute for X" brands. Use a breadcrumb system: allen institute/brain science, allen institute/immunology, etc. Describe your work based on what you work on, not ownership or hierarchy — a shift from "I am" to "I work on."

Past institutes are now accelerators; smaller efforts are projects or initiatives. The word "team" remains appropriate in casual contexts.
Can we still use acronyms (e.g., AIBS, AIFI)?
The goal is to reduce acronyms, especially in external communications. AIBS and AIFI should be phased out entirely.
Founder acknowledgement for research publications and posters
"This research was supported by the Allen Institute, founded by Jody Allen – chair and co-founder of Allen Family Philanthropies, and the late Paul G. Allen – investor, philanthropist, and co-founder of Microsoft. We gratefully acknowledge their vision and generosity, which make this work possible."
Presentation thank you slide and printed materials
"Thank you to our founders, Jody Allen and the late Paul G. Allen, whose vision and generosity make our work possible."
How to refer to our organization when submitting a manuscript for publication
Use a comma between Allen Institute and the name of your accelerator: Allen Institute, <Name of accelerator>
